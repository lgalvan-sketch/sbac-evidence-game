🔎 Your Mission

A 6th-grade student named Jane Garcia wrote a letter to her local newspaper arguing the community swimming pool should stay open. Her letter has a gap — one body paragraph makes a claim but doesn't have strong enough evidence to back it up.

Your job as an Evidence Detective is to read her letter, identify the weak spot, and choose the best sentence(s) to strengthen her argument.

⚡ Standard 6.W.1b — What You Need to Know
Good argument writers support their claim with relevant evidence. Evidence must:
  • Connect directly to the claim in that paragraph
  • Strengthen the argument (not just add info)
  • Stay on topic (not introduce unrelated ideas)

📄 Read Jane's Letter

Read carefully. Look for the paragraph that makes a claim but seems to be missing strong evidence.

Keep Municipal Pool Open

Dear Editor,

I am writing to argue that our local swimming pool should be kept open. I know that there are some who believe that we should close this pool and build two smaller pools, one on the north side and one on the south side of town. Although this sounds like a good idea, it doesn't address the value of our current community swimming pool.

First of all, the swimming pool is a real community pool. Because it is the only pool in town, everyone comes here to swim. This is where you meet your friends during the summer. Parents meet their friends here too. It provides our community with a common place for recreation and socialization.

Second, this swimming pool was built by the Works Project Administration (WPA). Almost every community received a new park, bridge, or school as a result of this agency, which put millions of people to work during the 1930s. The WPA built a swimming pool for our town. This is a part of our town's history, as well as our country's history.

Finally, our swimming pool is one of the largest pools in the area. It occupies one-half acre in the center of the town's park.

Our swimming pool has community, historical, and economic value. Please consider keeping it open.

Sincerely,
Jane Garcia

🔍 Detective Hint
Jane's conclusion mentions "economic value" — but do you see any evidence for that in the body paragraphs? Look especially at the final body paragraph (the "Finally" paragraph). Does it prove economic value?

🧩 Step 1 — Spot the Weak Paragraph

Jane's conclusion says the pool has "community, historical, and economic value."

Which paragraph is supposed to support economic value but doesn't give strong evidence for it?

🔬 Step 2 — Evaluate the Evidence Choices

Jane needs a sentence that supports economic value in the "Finally" paragraph. Read each option below. Click each one to analyze it — is it relevant, irrelevant, or off-topic?

People from surrounding towns come here to swim because our pool is so big and has three diving boards. Because many people choose to swim here, this has made money for our community that has helped maintain the pool and the entire park.
Although some people have complained that the pool is too big and takes up too much space in the park, this is not true. The park is large enough for other recreational activities, such as baseball and tennis.
One problem with such a big swimming pool is that it takes a long time for the water to warm. At the beginning of the summer, the water is so cold that it almost takes your breath away. By July, however, the water is much warmer.
Swimming is one of the best forms of exercise there is. It uses all of your muscle groups and burns many calories. More people in our community should consider swimming in our pool every day so that they can benefit.

✅ Step 3 — Final Answer

Based on your analysis, which sentence(s) give the best evidence to improve support for Jane's claim about the pool's economic value?

This is the real SBAC question — choose wisely!

✍️ Bonus: Write Your Own Evidence

You've found the right answer! Now practice the skill yourself. Jane's paragraph says the pool is "one of the largest pools in the area." Write 1–2 sentences of evidence that could support the pool's economic value.

💡 Evidence Starter Ideas
Think about: Does the pool bring money in? Does it save money? Does it create jobs? Does its size attract visitors?

📚 Key Takeaway — Standard 6.W.1b
When you write an argument, every body paragraph needs:
  1. A claim (what you're arguing)
  2. Evidence that directly supports THAT specific claim
  3. Evidence that is relevant and connected — not off-topic
Jane's "economic value" claim needed evidence showing the pool generates money — that's exactly what Choice A provided!
